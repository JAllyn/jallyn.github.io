NEW TO RUGBY?

Rugby 101

"Rugby is a game in which the object is to carry the ball over the opponent's goal line and force it to the ground to score."

Rugby Union (the version we play) is a fast-paced, physical, and highly strategic team sport played by two teams of 15 players. It is known for its emphasis on sportsmanship, respect for the referee, and the unique "ruck" and "maul" contests.

How to Score

5
Try
Grounding the ball in the opponent's in-goal area (try zone).
2
Conversion
Successful kick after a try (from a tee or drop kick).
3
Penalty Goal
Kick through the posts after an infringement by the opposition.
3
Drop Goal
Drop kick through the posts during open play (rare but exciting!).

Key Concepts & Rules

Offside

Players must stay behind the ball or the hindmost foot in rucks/mauls. Being offside is one of the most common infringements.

Ruck & Maul

When the ball carrier is tackled, a ruck forms. Players bind and compete for the ball. A maul is when the ball carrier is held but not brought to ground.

Tackle

Tackles must be below the shoulders. The tackled player must release the ball immediately. High tackles and dangerous play are penalized heavily.

Pro Tip from the Boars: Rugby is a game of possession, territory, and relentless effort. Fitness, teamwork, and smart decision-making matter as much as size and strength.
